# Community Code of Conduct

_Every member agrees to this before joining a meetup. It exists to keep our meetups warm, safe, and genuinely welcoming — especially for women and for anyone new in the city._

## The spirit
We bring together small groups of strangers over coffee (or chai) to make real, **platonic** connections. Come curious, come kind. Treat everyone the way you'd want a friend to be treated on their first day in a new place.

## What we expect
1. **Respect everyone.** No harassment, insults, or making anyone feel unwelcome — including on the basis of gender, faith, ethnicity, or background.
2. **Keep it platonic.** These are friendship and social meetups, not dating. No romantic or sexual advances. No unwanted flirting.
3. **Consent matters.** Ask before adding someone on socials, taking a photo of them, or sharing their contact. "No" is a complete answer.
4. **Protect privacy.** What people share at the table stays at the table. Don't share others' photos, numbers, or personal details without permission.
5. **Show up, on time.** Others planned their day around the group. If you can't make it, tell us as early as you can — repeated no-shows affect your ability to join future events.
6. **No selling or pushing.** No business pitching, MLM/network-marketing recruitment, or pressuring others about beliefs or politics.
7. **Stay present.** Be off your phone, in the conversation. You're here to meet people.

## Zero tolerance — immediate removal
- Any harassment, stalking, or unwanted advances (in person or in follow-up messages).
- Sharing or posting someone's private info or photos without consent.
- Recording anyone without their knowledge.
- Arriving intoxicated or bringing illegal substances.
- Threatening, discriminatory, or abusive behavior of any kind.

## How we handle issues
- **Report privately** anytime — message the organizer on WhatsApp, or use the "did anything feel unsafe?" question in the post-event form. Reports are confidential.
- You can **block** any member so you're never grouped with them again.
- Consequences scale: a warning → removal from the event → a permanent ban. Anything illegal is reported to the authorities.

## Safety basics
- All members are **CNIC-verified** before joining.
- Meetups are always in **public cafes**, never private homes.
- Trust your instincts. If something feels off, tell the organizer — we'd always rather you flag it.

---

_By joining, you agree to follow this Code of Conduct. Thank you for helping make this a place where anyone can walk in and feel they belong._
